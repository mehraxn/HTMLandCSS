My quiet study corner near the library

A student story about finding a small place on campus that made midterm week easier.

A

Alex Johnson

Computer Science student • Posted 2 days ago • 5 min read

During midterm week, the library was full and every table near the entrance was noisy. I walked to the back side of the building and found a small balcony with only two seats and a view of the courtyard.

It was not completely silent, but that was actually better for me. I could hear people walking outside, the fountain, and some soft campus noise. It made the place feel alive without becoming distracting.

“The best study place is not always the quietest place. It is the place where your brain understands that it is time to work.”

I now go there before web application labs to review my Flask notes, Bootstrap layout, and CSS classes. Small routines like this can make a big difference for students.
