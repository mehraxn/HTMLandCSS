Semantic HTML Made Simple

See what each tag is for by looking at the page itself.

<nav>

Navigation

Use this for menus and links that help people move around the site.

Go to Section Go to Article Go to Aside
<section>

This is a Section

A section is just a part of the page that groups related content together.

<article>

This is an Article

An article is a complete piece of content that can stand on its own, like a blog post or news story.

Example: This could be one full blog post inside the section. If you copied this article and placed it on another page, it would still make sense.

<article>

Another Article

A section can contain multiple articles when it groups similar standalone items.

<aside>

Extra Info

Use aside for side content that supports the main content but is not the main focus.

  • Related links
  • Tips
  • Author info
  • Ads or sidebar notes
Tip: article = full piece, section = grouped part, nav = menu, aside = extra info.
